Chapter 5: Creating Content

5.1 Introduction to Content Management

Term Definition
Page a web page on your Streamline site, containing text, images, or other content
File a resource on your computer that can be uploaded to your Files folder to use in pages
Image digital photos or animations in formats such as jpg, gif, or png, that can be uploaded to your Images folder to use in pages or teasers
Page Types Pre-built templates to simplify adding specific types of content to your site, such as Meeting-type or Profile-type pages

Special districts are responsible for posting a wide range of content to their web sites. The public demands information you have stored across text files and spreadsheets, in PDFs and tables, or in media like images and videos. You may not be sure how to best organize these varying items on your site.

The Universal Container

Worry not: everything you want to add to your Streamline site will be organized into its own little universal container, called a page, that can be easily plugged in wherever you want on the website ‚Äì in fact, if you want to feature just about any kind of content on your site in just about any important location, such as your homepage or in your menu, it must live on a page first.

Each Streamline page features a simple editor that lets you add formatted text, files, links, images, and more. Once your page is ready, you can save it and then plug it anywhere on the site you want as a teaser (more on organizing content later!)

Because Streamline works best when you think about giving everything its own page, this means you‚Äôll end up with many pages on your site. That‚Äôs OK! There are a variety of page types to assist with all the different types of content you may want to add.

So, where do you create all these new pages and edit existing ones? In the Content tab!

Key Concepts

  • Streamline simplifies managing the ‚Äústuff‚Äù on your site by requiring that everything be organized into its own container, called a page
  • In order to feature anything on your site for the public to find, like on your homepage or in your menu, you‚Äôll need to add it to a page first
  • Pages can be created and edited similarly to how you would use a basic word processor or email application. You‚Äôll see a complete list of your pages in the Content tab.

5.2 Content Tab: Pages

Term Definition
Filter toggle buttons that let you show / hide certain types of pages
Published / Unpublished status determining if a page is publicly viewable (unpublished pages are visible only to logged in users)
Clone feature that allows you to duplicate any page on your site

The Content tab is where all of the ‚Äústuff‚Äù on your site will live, organized into three principal folders: Pages, Images, and Files.

Clicking the Content tab opens the Pages folder automatically, where every single page on your site is visible in one place.

The Content Tab

To find existing pages, use the [B] search function ; if you want to see only specific type(s) of pages, you can use the [C] filter buttons. Once located, you can [E] edit any page by clicking on the pencil icon. If you want to create a new page, click the [D] +Add New button .

5.4 Adding Elements: images, files, and more

Term Definition
Elements Streamline term for discrete items on a page, such as images, videos, tables, which can be moved around the page or removed later
Options Each elements has options that can be configured while creating it, or by interacting with the element while editing a page

Want to add a photo, policy, agenda, application, or just about anything other than plain text to a page?

Then you‚Äôll need to know about elements: these are the images, files, tables, videos, or any other items that you might want to feature on a given page. Despite their diversity, elements have the advantage of all working pretty much the same way:

The Basic Element Flow

First, click a + icon to add a new element. You should see a menu of elements pop up.
These + icons run along the left-hand part of the Main Body Content area ‚Äì if you don‚Äôt see any, click outside the Content area and they should reappear.

Second, choose the type of element from the menu and click the green Insert button.
Depending on the type of element, you may need to configure more options (e.g. forms) or choose the content you want to show from your content folders (e.g. images or files).

Third, the element appears on the page!
Once you add an element, hover over it to see more options. You can also click on some elements to reconfigure them (such as image or form elements).

Element options

For example, you can hover your mouse over an image element to reveal icons that let you move, resize, remove, or add a hyperlink to any image on your page. Most elements have similar features visible when hovering or clicking on the element.

Reviewing the Element menu

The most commonly-used elements on Streamline pages are images [1] & files [3]; it‚Äôs important to note that these elements pull directly from their respective folders in the Content tab (5.2). You can either pull from existing images or files in those folders, or you can upload new ones from your computer ‚Äúon-the-fly‚Äù.

For a step-by-step look at using image and file elements, see the example below
You‚Äôll need to know about these elements specifically to complete the activity for this chapter.

However, there are many other elements to learn about and use.
For example:
  • Form elements allow you to create web forms that can be filled out online by the public, with submissions assigned to a staff member
  • Image galleries are ideal for creating slideshows of multiple images on a single page
  • Video elements can embed any YouTube or Vimeo video and allow them to be played on your site
  • HTML elements allow for any code to be run on the page, including 3rd party plugins (for advanced users)

If you want to look ahead, you can learn more about the other elements from our Knowledge Base reference guide.

Activity: Create an ‚ÄúAbout Us‚Äù Page

Introduction

One of the most important pages on a district site is the ‚ÄúAbout Us‚Äù page: it gives the district an opportunity to introduce itself to the public, who may not know much about your district or the services you provide (and may be skeptical about where their tax money is going!)

Goal

Typically, an About Us page will include a short history of your district, a list of the basic services you provide, fun facts, or just about anything you want. This page is a chance to express your district‚Äôs personality as well as its purpose, so it should be informative, engaging, and work to form a positive introduction to the public.

For some inspiration, check out these examples of great About Us pages on Streamline sites:

  • Bayshore Gardens: https://www.bayshoregardens.org/about-us
  • Paradise Rec & Park: https://www.paradiseprpd.com/about-us
  • Delta Diablo: https://www.deltadiablo.org/about-us
  • Mt. View Sanitary District: https://www.mvsd.org/about-us

Instructions

  1. Create a new Standard page on your Streamline site
  2. Title the page ‚ÄúAbout Us‚Äù
  3. Add at least four (4) of the following to the Main Body Content area:
  • Short history of the district
  • A document related to your district
  • Services provided (must use bulleted or numbered lists)
  • District ‚Äúfun facts‚Äù (must use bulleted or numbered lists)
  • Quote from your GM / mission statement (must use Quote style)
  • Image of something related to your district
  • Something critical for people to know (must use one of the heading or callout styles)
  • Extra Credit: A link to Streamline (www.getstreamline.com)!

Tips & Takeaways

  • If you have multiple team members completing this activity, you can create multiple About Us pages by adding your last names to the title section from step 2 above. You can vote for a favorite before going live.
  • The examples in the goal section all employ some (or all) of the types of content required for this activity ‚Äì don‚Äôt be afraid to copy them!
  • If you get stuck, you can also refer to examples below for step-by-step visuals for how to add each of the types of content required for this activity.
